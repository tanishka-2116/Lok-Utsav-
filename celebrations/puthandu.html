Puthandu

The Tamil New Year of Renewal and Reflection

Puthandu, also known as Tamil Puthandu, is the traditional New Year festival of the Tamil people. It is celebrated on the first day of the Tamil month Chithirai, usually falling in mid-April.

The word “Puthandu” means “new year”, symbolising fresh beginnings, hope, gratitude, and reflection. The festival aligns with the Sun’s entry into the zodiac sign of Aries (Mesha).

Mythological and Cultural Significance

According to Tamil tradition, Puthandu is associated with Lord Brahma, the creator of the universe. It is believed that creation began on this day, making it a cosmic new beginning.

The festival also marks the start of a new agricultural and solar cycle, reflecting ancient Tamil knowledge of astronomy, timekeeping, and nature.

Puthandu Kani – The Auspicious First Sight

Similar to Vishu, the first sight of the year (Kani) holds great importance. Families arrange auspicious items on the previous night so they are the first thing seen on Puthandu morning.

Items in Puthandu Kani

  • Mirror (self-awareness and truth)
  • Gold or coins (prosperity)
  • Fruits and flowers
  • Betel leaves and nuts
  • Sacred texts or images of deities
  • Traditional oil lamp (Kuthuvilakku)

Panchanga Reading (Panchanga Vaachanam)

One of the most important rituals of Puthandu is the reading of the Tamil Panchangam, which outlines astrological predictions for the coming year.

Elders or priests read the Panchangam aloud to the family, helping people prepare mentally and spiritually for the year ahead.

Traditional Food – Maanga Pachadi

The signature dish of Puthandu is Maanga Pachadi, a symbolic preparation reflecting the philosophy of life.

Ingredients & Meaning

  • Raw mango: Sourness – unexpected moments
  • Jaggery: Sweetness – happiness and success
  • Neem flowers: Bitterness – challenges and hardships
  • Chili: Spiciness – anger and passion
  • Mustard: Sharpness – alertness and wisdom

This dish teaches acceptance of life in all its flavours, encouraging balance and maturity.

Rituals and Celebrations

  • Early Morning Oil Bath: Symbolises purification and renewal.
  • Temple Visits: Devotees visit temples to seek blessings.
  • Exchange of Greetings: People greet each other saying “Puthandu Vazthukal”.
  • Family Feasts: Traditional vegetarian meals are shared.

Traditional Attire

Wearing new clothes on Puthandu symbolises the start of a fresh chapter in life.

  • Women: Silk sarees like Kanchipuram sarees
  • Men: Veshti (dhoti) with shirt or angavastram
  • Children: New festive clothing

The Message of Puthandu

Puthandu teaches that life begins anew when we approach it with clarity, acceptance, and gratitude. By honouring both joy and struggle, the festival prepares individuals for a balanced and meaningful year.
