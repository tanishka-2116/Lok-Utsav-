Durga Puja

The Festival of Power, Protection, and People

Durga Puja is the most important and widely celebrated festival of the Bengali community. It commemorates the victory of Goddess Durga over the buffalo demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.

More than a religious observance, Durga Puja is a massive cultural phenomenon marked by elaborate pandals, artistic idol-making, music, dance, food, and a unique “Sarbojonin” spirit — a celebration meant for everyone.

Durga Puja 2026 – Key Dates

  • Mahalaya: 16 October – Invocation of the Goddess
  • Maha Shasthi: 17 October – Bodhan (awakening of the Goddess)
  • Maha Saptami: 18 October – Nabapatrika Snan
  • Maha Ashtami: 19 October – Sandhi Puja and Pushpanjali
  • Maha Navami: 20 October – Final day of worship
  • Vijaya Dashami: 21 October – Sindoor Khela and Visarjan

The Deeper Meaning of Durga Puja

Goddess Durga represents Shakti, the divine feminine energy that protects the universe from chaos and injustice. Her battle with Mahishasura symbolizes the eternal struggle between righteousness and ego.

Durga Puja teaches that evil is not destroyed by brute force alone, but through courage, wisdom, discipline, and unity. The festival also celebrates womanhood, strength, and moral responsibility.

A Sacred Story from the Scriptures

According to the Devi Mahatmya, Mahishasura received a boon that he could not be killed by any man or god. Drunk with power, he began terrorizing heaven and earth.

The gods combined their energies to create Goddess Durga, who rode a lion and wielded divine weapons. After a fierce battle lasting nine nights, Durga slew Mahishasura at the moment of Sandhi — the sacred junction of Ashtami and Navami.

This moment is worshipped as Sandhi Puja, one of the most powerful rituals of the festival.

Key Rituals and Practices

Bodhan and Mahashasthi

Bodhan marks the ceremonial awakening of Goddess Durga, inviting her presence into the idol.

Nabapatrika Snan

On Saptami, nine sacred plants are bathed and worshipped, representing nine forms of the Goddess and her connection with nature.

Pushpanjali and Sandhi Puja

Devotees offer flowers through Pushpanjali, while Sandhi Puja marks the exact cosmic moment when Durga defeated Mahishasura.

Sindoor Khela

On Vijaya Dashami, married women smear each other with vermillion (sindoor), praying for marital happiness and prosperity, before bidding farewell to the Goddess.

Visarjan

The idols are immersed in rivers or water bodies, symbolizing the cyclical nature of creation and dissolution.

Art, Music, and Cultural Expression

Durga Puja is renowned for its artistic pandals, each designed around unique themes ranging from mythology to social awareness.

Dhunuchi Naach, performed with smoking clay incense burners to the beats of the Dhak, is a powerful visual and spiritual expression.

In 2021, Durga Puja in Kolkata was recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

Traditional Attire

For Women

Women traditionally wear white sarees with red borders such as Garad or Korial. Jamdani, Baluchari, and Tant sarees are popular. Married women wear Shankha-Pola bangles.

For Men

Men usually wear cotton or silk Kurtas (Punjabi) paired with dhoti or pajama.

Food, Bhog, and Festive Flavours

Ashtami Bhog

The sacred bhog served on Ashtami is vegetarian and includes Bhoger Khichuri, Labra, Beguni, tomato chutney, and Payesh.

Sweets

Traditional sweets such as Sandesh, Rosogolla, and Narkel Naru are shared among families and visitors.

Non-Vegetarian Delicacies

Outside the pandals, the festival is famous for Kosha Mangsho and fish dishes like Bhetki Paturi.

Social and Cultural Message

Durga Puja celebrates collective participation, artistic freedom, and inclusivity. The Sarbojonin nature of the festival makes it a shared experience beyond religion.

It reminds society of the power of unity, respect for women, and the responsibility to stand against injustice.

Durga Puja is not merely a festival — it is a living expression of faith, art, and people’s power. Through devotion, creativity, and togetherness, Durga Puja continues to shape cultural identity and collective consciousness.
